📋 DESIGN SEQUENCE GUIDE — PARAMETER SELECTION ORDER
STEP 1: TUBE OD (Do) -
Recommended: 19mm (3/4") for general service. 25mm for viscous/fouling fluids. 12.7mm for high pressure/compact design.
STEP 2: TUBE ID (Di) & WALL THICKNESS -
Di = Do - 2 × wall_thickness. Recommended Di = Do - 3mm. Affects tube velocity and Re.
STEP 3: TUBE LAYOUT -
Triangular: 15-30% more tubes, higher HTC (clean fluids). Square: easier cleaning (fouling fluids).
STEP 4: PITCH RATIO (Pt/Do) -
TEMA minimum: 1.25 × Do. Higher pitch = lower pressure drop but lower heat transfer.
STEP 5: TUBE LENGTH (L) -
Standard lengths: 3000, 4000, 6000, 7270mm. L/Ds ratio = 5-10 for good design.
STEP 6: TUBE PASSES (Np) -
Higher Np = higher tube velocity & HTC, but higher pressure drop. Even numbers only.
STEP 7: BAFFLE SPACING (B) -
Rule: 0.2×Ds ≤ B ≤ Ds. Optimum: 0.3-0.5 × Ds for best HTC/ΔP ratio.
STEP 8: SHELL ID (Ds) -
Always round UP to nearest standard TEMA shell ID step.

TECHNICAL EVALUATIONS DATASHEET — STHE DESIGN
⚠ COOLING WATER OUTLET TEMPERATURE EXCEEDS 40°C
Current exit temp: - | Limit: 40°C

REASON: At temperatures above 40°C, solubility of dissolved salts (CaCO₃, MgSO₄) in water decreases. Decreasing solubility leads to precipitation, causing mineral deposits (scale) to form on the heat transfer surface, which increases fouling resistance and degrades U over time.

CONSEQUENCE: Scale deposition on tube walls reduces effective heat transfer area. Can cause tube blockage, corrosion under deposit, and reduced exchanger life.

RECOMMENDATION: Increase cooling water flow rate to reduce outlet temp below 40°C.
